Trump likes to go where he's welcome

President Trump puts America first even when it comes to where he travels.

Elena Cox & Satu Helin

Published December 14, 2019

When President Donald Trump kicked off his campaign in 2015, he pledged to put America first. Based on where he's traveled since he's taken office, we see he took that message literally. Trump frequently travels to cities throughout the United States but has had fewer trips abroad.

Part of the reason may be because he doesn't like to sleep anywhere but his own bed. Throughout his presidency, he often returns to one of his residences in New York, Washington or Florida after holding a Make America Great Again rally.

Making America Great Again

Trump rallies since he's taken office.

From February 2017 to November 2019, President Trump has held 72 MAGA rallies across the U.S.

According to data obtained from Morning Consult, President Trump prefers to visit states where he's popular. He's held rallies in 44 states that viewed him positively at the time of his visit, versus 25 that viewed him negatively.

Based on where he's held rallies, it seems Trump feels most comfortable in the Southern U.S. He's held the fewest rallies in his home region of the Northeast.

President Trump has held more rallies in the U.S. than his recent predecesors George W. Bush and Barack Obama. But after 34 months in office, he has spent less time abroad than previous presidents in the same amount of time. He not only went on a fewer number of trips, but spent less days away from the U.S.

Trump Avoids Key Western Allies

When he does go abroad, Trump has frequently traveled to East Asia but has avoided some key western allies According to Pew Research, Trump has generally been viewed negatively outside the U.S. During his administration, he has visited many countries where he is liked, such as Israel and Vietnam.

President Trump has held bilateral meetings in 10 different countries since he took office.

Trump is unpopular almost everywhere in Europe. Of the places he has traveled, his popularity was highest in Eastern Asia and Israel. Countries in green have no data on popularity.

Normally, U.S. presidents hold bilateral meetings in Germany, Canada and Mexico. So far, Trump has not visited any of those countries. In Germany, his popularity rate was 9, in Mexico it was 6 and in Canada it was 22 in 2018, the most recent data available.

Trump is not totally different from his predecessors when it comes to traveling. Like them, he did the biggest bulk of his traveling during his first year in office. After that, the phase has slowed down.
